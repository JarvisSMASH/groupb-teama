WHY DO WE WANT TO SOLVE THIS PROBLEM?

The Southeast has the highest rates of energy poverty of any region in the country, a situation compounded by its comparative lack of initiatives to address the many effects that stem from that fact. Low-income families already living in financial poverty to experience a disproportionately high monthly expenditure on energy.
If there was a way to reduce the high cost of energy in metro atlanta, then people would could use there income on other basic needs such as medicine and food. As alredy previously stated: Energy poverty has become so severe in the Southeast that many households pay 600 percent more of their annual income on energy than the national average.

Our Solutions

Stream Solar Panel

The Stream Solar Panel uses both solar and hydroelectric energy; they would be put in lakes or ponds and are used to generate energy for communities. The solar panel and water turbine run simultaneously, both energy sources go to different homes similar to a wind energy farm.

Rain Turbine

Works Simililar to the Stream Solar Panel in that it's powered by water flowing through it, but intead of it flowing through it horizonaly, the water flows thorigh it vertically. As rain pours on the roof of the house it is collected thorigh the gutter system and pured trough the Rain Turbine where the Mechanical energy caused by moter spinning is turned into electrical energy. The remaining water is then filtered and used for the home.
